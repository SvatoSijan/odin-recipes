Idly Sambar

Idly sambar is a classic South Indian dish featuring steamed rice cakes (idly) served with a flavorful and tangy lentil-based stew (sambar). It's a wholesome and satisfying meal enjoyed for breakfast or as a snack.

Now let's make a delicious plate of idly sambar.

  • Ingredients
    1. Idly (steamed rice cakes)
    2. Sambar powder
    3. Toor dal (pigeon peas)
    4. Tamarind paste
    5. Vegetables (e.g., carrots, beans, drumsticks)
    6. Mustard seeds
    7. Curry leaves
    8. Asafoetida (hing)
    9. Turmeric powder
    10. Oil
    11. Salt (to taste)
  • Let's Cook
    1. Cook toor dal until soft and set aside.
    2. In a pot, heat oil, add mustard seeds, curry leaves, and asafoetida. Let them splutter.
    3. Add chopped vegetables and saut√© until slightly tender.
    4. Add tamarind paste, sambar powder, turmeric powder, and salt. Mix well.
    5. Pour in water and let the mixture simmer until the vegetables are fully cooked.
    6. Add the cooked toor dal to the pot and mix thoroughly. Adjust the consistency with water if needed.
    7. Allow the sambar to boil for a few minutes to let the flavors meld.
    8. Steam the idlies and serve them hot with the prepared sambar.
  • Enjoy Your Idly Sambar!
