Alu Pakoda

Alu pakora is a fried potato dish which is 'hot and crispy' on the outside and 'hot and soft' on the inside. It can be enjoyed all year round, but it is DIVINE on a rainy and/or foggy day. Pair it with a chai, and you are good to go.

Now let's make the darn thing.

  • Ingredients
    1. Potato
    2. Onion
    3. Gram Flour
    4. Cumin Seeds
    5. Indian Chat Masala
    6. Corn Starch
  • Let's Cook
    1. Wash and peel the potatoes. Then grind them through a mesh. Make sure the mesh is not fine. We want the potatoes a little THICK. Cut the onions finely. Mix both of them and add a little corn flour.
    2. Make a batter by adding 2 cups of Gram Flour with 2 cups of water. Add the water slowly and mix the flour. We want the batter to be on the runny side. Now add 2 tablespoons of cumin seed and add salt according to your preference.
    3. Take a deep dish skillet and put in vegetable or peanut oil. Put it on the gas stove and turn on the gas. We want a high temp. Now make bite-size balls of our potato mixture and coat them with the batter. Then fry them until golden brown, remove them. They will continue cooking until they get a little brown. Then sprinkle them with Indian Chat Masala.
    4. Now enjoy your crispy pakodas with a side of ketchup or mint chutney if you can.
  • Enjoy!
